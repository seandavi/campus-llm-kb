### 1. Argument spine

Institutional trust is the "precondition" for effective AI deployment, not a byproduct of it. While technical performance is a driver of acceptance, patients—especially from historically marginalized communities—view clinical AI through a lens of historical mistrust and concerns about algorithmic bias. Social license is earned through meaningful transparency (human-in-the-loop guarantees), participatory design (co-creation with patients), and robust governance structures like Patient AI Advisory Councils. AMCs that fail to distinguish between "informing" a community and "involving" them in governance risk a "transparency paradox," where disclosure without engagement actually erodes trust. Ultimately, the durability of AI in the clinic depends on whether patients believe the system is designed to serve them rather than just optimize institutional efficiency.
